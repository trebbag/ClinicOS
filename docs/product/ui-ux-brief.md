# Clinic OS UI/UX Brief

## What this experience is trying to optimize

Clinic OS should help leadership teams understand clinic readiness and risk quickly, then act with the right controls. The experience is meant to optimize for:

- fast oversight
- clear operational confidence
- review before publish
- role-aware visibility
- non-PHI governance clarity

This is not a general-purpose EHR-like workspace. It is an operating system for clinic governance, oversight, and controlled follow-through.

## Why the app uses a unified command center

Clinic work crosses roles constantly. Office operations, quality, training, standards, revenue, and deployment health all affect one another. A unified command center is the best fit because it:

- gives one shared source of truth
- reduces role silos
- keeps leadership aligned on the same priorities
- lets multi-role operators move between responsibilities without leaving the product shell

Instead of building a different homepage for every role, the app should keep one consistent command center and reveal the right modules based on capability and role context.

## How different leaders benefit

### Executives

- See the overall health of the clinic quickly.
- Understand where risk is building across operations, quality, staffing, and commercial governance.
- Review what is waiting for approval or publication without hunting through multiple tools.

### Office leaders

- Run the day from one place.
- Track room readiness, checklist performance, issues, escalations, and Planner follow-through.
- See both today’s operations and emerging readiness patterns over time.

### Quality leaders

- Move smoothly between incidents, CAPAs, standards, evidence binders, committees, QAPI trends, and evidence-gap remediation.
- Keep remediation, verification, and committee follow-through visible in one flow.

### HR leads

- See performance, recurring training, overdue cycles, and coaching burden together.
- Spot repeated requirement failures early instead of reacting after audits or reviews.

## What “good” looks like for rollout and adoption

Good adoption does not mean every user learns every workflow. It means:

- the command center becomes the trusted daily oversight view
- leaders know where to look for urgent work
- approvals and publications feel controlled and understandable
- operational issues can be diagnosed from the app itself
- no one needs separate shadow spreadsheets to understand status

## UX principles stakeholders should align on

- Draft-first: generated work starts as draft until reviewed.
- Oversight before action: summaries and risk visibility come before editing.
- One command center: one shell, many workspaces.
- Role-aware visibility: show the right work without fragmenting the product.
- Safety and clarity: approval and publish actions must always be explicit.
- Desktop-first: the app should work best where real clinic oversight work happens.

## The intended feel

The product should feel:

- calm under pressure
- operationally credible
- clearly governed
- faster than spreadsheets and scattered tools
- trustworthy enough that leaders can use it as the clinic’s control surface

## What the next UI/UX phase should deliver

- a strong command-center homepage
- consistent workspace patterns across operations, quality, people, revenue, and standards
- clearer analytics modules and trend summaries
- a refined Pilot Ops experience that makes rollout and runtime state easy to understand
